21 June 2018

Chinsky updated:

  • Disarm chances now both affected by skills and anything tha affects melee accuracy (being blind, being confused, blurry eyes, being in pain etc).

afterthought, sabiram updated:

  • The hydroponics station away site has been converted into an exoplanet site.

20 June 2018

BlueNexus updated:

  • Railguns have been rebalanced to fill the niche of an anti-armour or sniper type role.
  • All railguns now suffer a substantial penalty when fired one-handed.
  • The basic railgun projectile no longer stuns, and does less damage.
  • All railguns which accept steel rods as ammunition now use the basic projectile.
  • The TCC railgun's (available in the traitor menu) ammo capacity has been reduced from 10 to 6.
  • Substantially increased firing delay on all semi-automatic coilguns and railguns.
  • Holding or stowing a railgun now slows you down more.

Chinsky updated:

  • EVA skill now has mechanical effects.
  • Space slipping chance is affected by EVA skill. Bit higher at unskilled, lesser with skill, no slipping at experienced.
  • Flooring when entering gravity from space is now not guaranteed at Basic skill or more. Doesn't happen at Professional skill.
  • At Unskilled and Basic skill, jetpack can sometimes go wrong way. Not that bad if you notice in time and have fuel to turn back.
  • At Professional skill you go faster when in space with a jetpack. Zooom.
  • Skills now affect surgery. Trained or Experienced (depending on what you're trying to do) skill is needed to ensure step won't fail. Professional skill will offset various penalties (not proper optable, improvised tools etc) a bit. Time of step is affected by Anatomy skill too.
  • Some other skills help with some steps, e.g. Trained Forensics offsets penalty for incisions, and Complex Devices skill is used instead for robotic organ steps, with Electrician and Construction helping with simple limb repair steps.

Chisnky updated:

  • Melee weapons now have different attack cooldowns, generally with smaller weapons being faster, bigger slower. Normal sized weapons are baseline, having same attack cooldown as they did.
  • Material weapons further tweak their attack cooldown based on material used.

afterthought2 updated:

  • The leadership skill has been removed. Check your characters' skill allocations.
  • The instruct verb works like before, but no longer requires leadership skill to use.

chaoko99 updated:

  • You may no longer spam the skeleton as hard as you could before.
  • Wall painters no longer hit walls when used.
  • Energy axes will no longer anchor when dropped.
  • Candles will now last about 30 minutes.
  • Bucklers can now block any projectiles that aren't bullets, as suggested intent in their code.
  • You may no longer worry about reagent contamination in a soft drink dispenser, only unintended reagent amounts.
  • You may light an unlit candle with a lit candle that isn't in your hand.
  • Optimization pass on Geiger counters, and a few effect systems (Smoke and sparks)
  • Underwear may now go into washing machines. Still can't be bloodied, but at least you can do that now. For you weird people who roleplay showering!
  • Rags may now clean more than just blood. More importantly, they show their progress bar when cleaning.

Zuhayr updated:

  • Gas inhalation effects are now reagent-based. Gases create reagents inside the body, which then apply effects. This means that gaseous phoron exposure, for example, needs to be treated with dialysis/dylovene, as it fills your guts up with liquid phoron.
  • The poison_type var on species datums is now poison_types, and determines which poison gasses show you the toxins UI indicator, rather than which ones cause poisoning. The actual effects of poisonous gasses is determined by the reagent value (as per dexalin being injected into a vox, etc.). This really shouldn't impact anything for the average player, except that chlorine and nitrogen dioxide (not N2O, for clarity) are now actually poisonous.
  • Helium now makes your voice squeaky. Very important feature.
  • Xenon is now functionally the same as N2O, including the following note.
  • N2O now causes giggling, confusion, dizziness and occasional passing out at high dosages, rather than instant and immediate knockout. Still useful for riot control and now flooding it into distro isn't a round-ender. You might even be able to use it without admins shouting at you! Refer to the optable neural suppressor PR for a replacement method to sedate people for surgery.
  • Finally, as a sidenote, Bogani will actually be poisoned by oxygen and chlorine will have a visible overlay now.
